The Challenge

Retro-directive array (RDA) has been considered a good candidate for beamforming and has well-established efficiency in wireless communication systems for years. One of the most important components in the RF system for the RDA is a circulator. However, the isolation characteristic of circulator between transmitter (TX) output and receiver (RX) input affects the performance of the array system not only in terms of sensitivity but also beamforming accuracy. There have been many recent reports performing leakage signal cancelation, typically in the antenna interface and RF both in frequency and time domains. However, in terms of bandwidth and isolation, those are still not efficient and compatible for the retro-directive short-range WPT system.

IC Design & Verification

We propose the use of phase conjugate technique which is preferable to realize the power transmission retrodirective radar system. The operating frequency is chosen at 2.4 GHz, which is defined for industrial, scientific and medical (ISM) using. The phase conjugating circuit will be implemented by commercial devices as well as CMOS technology. The phase conjugating circuitry contains five blocks such as voltage controlled oscillator (VCO), low noise amplifier (LNA), mixer,power amplifier (PA).

Mixer plays a major role in translating input RF signal down to the IF signal of interest. This mixer circuit employs a well-known double balance Gilbert-cell on which has a differential input sensing signal from LNA, LO signal generated by VCO and the differential IF at output. The signal after being translated by mixeris not sufficiently large to convey over the long distance. Thus, magnifying the IF signal to a level is a major job that the PA at transmitting path should involve in

Due to the imperfect isolation of circulator, a large TX leakage signal at 2.4 GHz is fed into RX amplifiers to be responsible for the interference of 2.5 GHz at TX that again falls into a loop and contributes another leakage at 2.5 GHz Using surface acoustic wave (SAW) filters to reduce the leakage signals is expensive, bulky, and incompatible with CMOS integration. Hence, a proposal of 200-MHz Circulator Leakage Canceller (CLC) is our target to serve for leakage suppression in current RDA system as well as could be applied to other systems having a similar frequency plan.
