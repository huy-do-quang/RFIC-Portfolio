Design, simulate, and tape-out RF front-end ICs for a wireless power transmission system (WPTS) employing phase conjugating retro-directive array. RF ICs includes a Low Noise Amplifier (LNA), Mixer, and Drive Amplifier (DA).

The Challenge

Retro-directive array (RDA) has been considered a good candidate for beamforming and has well-established efficiency in wireless communication systems for years. One of the most important components in the RF system for the RDA is a circulator. However, the isolation characteristic of circulator between transmitter (TX) output and receiver (RX) input affects the performance of the array system not only in terms of sensitivity but also beamforming accuracy. There have been many recent reports performing leakage signal cancelation, typically in the antenna interface and RF both in frequency and time domains. However, in terms of bandwidth and isolation, those are still not efficient and compatible for the retro-directive short-range WPT system.

IC Design & Verification

We propose the use of phase conjugate technique which is preferable to realize the power transmission retrodirective radar system. The operating frequency is chosen at 2.4 GHz, which is defined for industrial, scientific and medical (ISM) using. The phase conjugating circuit will be implemented by commercial devices as well as CMOS technology. The phase conjugating circuitry contains five blocks such as voltage controlled oscillator (VCO), low noise amplifier (LNA), mixer,power amplifier (PA).

Mixer plays a major role in translating input RF signal down to the IF signal of interest. This mixer circuit employs a well-known double balance Gilbert-cell on which has a differential input sensing signal from LNA, LO signal generated by VCO and the differential IF at output. The signal after being translated by mixeris not sufficiently large to convey over the long distance. Thus, magnifying the IF signal to a level is a major job that the PA at transmitting path should involve in

Due to the imperfect isolation of circulator, a large TX leakage signal at 2.4 GHz is fed into RX amplifiers. Using SAW filters to reduce the leakage signals is expensive, bulky, and incompatible with CMOS integration. Hence, a proposal of Circulator Leakage Canceller (CLC) is our target to serve for leakage suppression in current RDA system as well as could be applied to other systems having a similar frequency plan. The proposed CLC consists of a directional coupler (DC), a hybrid coupler, and a low-pass filter network (LPFN) integrated with Negative Capacitor (NCAP).

Simulation Results

Simulation of Mixer shows the P1dB of -23 dBm while AM-PM is roughly 0.1 degree/dB. This P1dB point shows that for each an increase of 20 dB input power from one end of mixer, the phase locating in IF signal is modulated by approximate 2 degress from the other end. LNA with the drive amplifier (DA) circuit has the gain of 43 dB which can push the small signal sensed by antenna up to some extents such that it goes beyond the P1dB point of -23 dBm and falls into the saturation region in mixer.


The feedforward path is first designed with an attenuator and phase shifter, such that the RF CLC can perform high cancelation at every single frequency in the bandwidth of interest. The set of achieved phase shifts and attenuations at individual points provides the combined responses as the solid lines. The simulation shows that with NCAP = −2 pF, both phase and magnitude LPFN offer the best matching with requirements.



To prevent LPFN from process voltage temperature (PVT) variations, NCAP was designed with CL metal insulator metal (MIM) capacitor banks to provide phase tunability of approximately 50 degrees. Because of the frequencyplanning of the retro-directive RF system, the LPFN was designed to obtain the first- and second-best isolation at 2.5 and 2.4 GHz, respectively, to improve minimum sensitivity, mitigate linearity requirements, and reduce filtering burden in the RX.

Testbenches and Chip Measurement

The RDS was fabricated using Samsung LR6LP 65-nm RF CMOS technology. Beside shows a photograph of the fabricated chips.








The measured isolation characteristics at 2.5 and 2.4 GHz are −45 and −42 dB, respectively. De-embedding the intrinsic isolation of the circulator, the leakage cancelation at 2.5 and 2.4 GHz is 24 and 22.5 dB, respectively. Measurement shows the measured P1dB of CLC of approximately 16 dBm.





Mixer's performance measurements were made with RF, IF, and LO frequencies of 2.5 GHz, 2.4 GHz, and 4.9 GHz. The magnitude difference between the 2.4-GHz and 2.5-GHz components at the output is more than 26 dB, which guarantees RMS jitter of less than 4.3 ps.

Results and Reflection:

  • A wideband CLC for a retro-directive RF system was proposed. An NCAP with the wideband flattening phase was employed in the CLC to improve the circulator’s isolation, which enhances minimum sensitivity, linearity requirement, and filtering burden of the RF system.
  • An ultra-compact CMOS phase conjugator is presented, provides variable gain and linear AM-PM characteristics, yielding a constant output power and phase across a specific input power range.
